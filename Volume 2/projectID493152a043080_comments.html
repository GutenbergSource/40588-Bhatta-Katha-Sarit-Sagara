A huge collection of ancient Indian stories, translated from Sanskrit.

This text contains a lot letters with diacritic marks. Please proof them following the standard guidelines for diacritics. I've kept the resolution of the scans high, so that you can enlarge to determine the exact diacritic used, if necessary.

Sometimes, the S with acute accent looks like an S followed by a prime. Also in this case, please proof it as if it is an S with acute accent, that is, as ['S].

Section headers are set into the text in italics. Please move this text, and place it above the paragraph it belongs to, as if it is a section header.

The Sanskrit names in this work are not yet in the word-check dictionary. Please use the word-check interface in P1 to suggest those names for inclusion.

The scans of this book are available from The Internet Archive. A few pages are difficult to read, due to blurring or to folded edges. Please notify me if you cannot resolve the text, and I will help in finding out the correct reading.

Original Scans: Volume 1 (Books I to IX), Volume 2 (Books X to XVIII).

A later edition, which we cannot clear under PG rules, is available here: Volume 1 (Books I and II), Volume 2 (Books III to V), Volume 3 (Books VI and VII), Volume 4 (Book VIII), Volume 5 (Books X and XI), Volume 6 (Book XII), Volume 7 (Book XII, continued), Volume 8 (Books XIII to XVII), Volume 9 (Book XVIII), Volume 10 (Additional Material).

Please only use this later version to resolve unreadable sections. Do not copy entire pages from it. Note that this later edition uses macrons where the older edition uses acute accents. Also, this version is restructured, and published in ten instead of two volumes.
